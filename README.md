## What I build in the open

**AI for the enterprise — built to be trusted, and measured**

- 🔐 **[zero-trust-mcp-gateway](https://github.com/nickbiird/zero-trust-mcp-gateway)** — *Enterprises won't let AI agents near their data until they can trust them with it.* This shows how that trust is earned: an AI agent only ever sees what the person asking is allowed to see — never more. Underneath it's an identity-and-access layer (OAuth 2.1 + attribute-based access control + database row-level security); what matters is the result, proven and re-checked automatically — **100% of 698 adversarial attempts denied, zero false alarms, no measurable slowdown.** *My flagship.*
- 🪜 **[authority-ladder](https://github.com/nickbiird/authority-ladder)** — *Most AI-transformation budgets get spent on the wrong use cases.* Drop in a backlog of AI ideas and get back a defended, **priced** roadmap: is this even an AI problem, how much autonomy is safe, what's the architecture, what's the regulatory risk, and what does each one cost to run — every call reviewed and held for human sign-off before anything commits.
- ⚖️ **[ai-act-triage](https://github.com/nickbiird/ai-act-triage)** — *"Which of our forty AI use cases actually needs the lawyer?"* Describe one in plain language and get a **cited EU AI Act risk tier** that's been through an adversarial check and a human gate — in seconds, for cents, so the legal budget goes where the real risk is. Triage, not legal advice — and that distinction is the point.
